About the Study

Shopping Mall 1999 is an interactive narrative game created as part of a doctoral research study at the University of Central Florida's Texts & Technology program.

This study explores how interactive storytelling tools can be used to teach foundational coding concepts to novice users. The game was built using Twine's Chapbook story format — a platform specifically designed with accessibility and ease of use in mind.

Participants are asked to complete a short pre-survey, play through the game, and finish with a brief post-survey. The entire experience takes approximately 45–60 minutes. Your responses are confidential and will be used solely for academic research purposes.

About the Author

Ricky Finch is a PhD candidate in the Texts & Technology program at the University of Central Florida. His research sits at the intersection of digital humanities, interactive narrative, and pedagogical design.

His dissertation examines Twine's Chapbook story format as a platform for teaching coding concepts through interactive fiction, using platform studies methodology to analyze how Chapbook's design shapes both the creation and reception of interactive narrative.

When he is not writing about abandoned malls, he works as a writing consultant and is guided through his dissertation by a telepathic cat named Oliver.
