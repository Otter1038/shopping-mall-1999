About the Study

An Interactive Narrative Research Study

Shopping Mall 1999 is an interactive narrative game created as part of a doctoral research study at the University of Central Florida's Texts & Technology program.

This study explores how interactive storytelling tools — specifically Twine's Chapbook story format — can be used to teach foundational coding concepts to novice users. The game was designed with accessibility in mind, using minimal code syntax and a mobile-friendly format to lower barriers for both creators and learners.

Participants are asked to complete the following:

I. Pre-Survey — A short questionnaire about your prior experience with coding and interactive fiction. Takes approximately 5 minutes.
II. Play the Game — Explore Shopping Mall 1999 at your own pace. The game takes approximately 30–40 minutes to complete.
III. Post-Survey — A follow-up questionnaire reflecting on the experience. Takes approximately 5–10 minutes.

The entire session should take approximately 45–60 minutes. Your responses are confidential and will be used solely for academic research purposes in accordance with UCF's IRB guidelines.

If you have any questions about the study before participating, please reach out directly.
