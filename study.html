About the Study

Explanation of Research

Title of Study: “Teaching Creative Coding Through Narrative: A Study of Twine’s Chapbook Format and Story-Based Learning”

Principal Investigator: Richard Finch

Faculty Supervisor: Dr. Emily Johnson

You are invited to take part in a research study. Whether you take part is up to you.

The purpose of this research is to learn how you understand various coding skills and learn coding by playing an interactive story game made in Twine’s Chapbook format. Twine is software that helps users create and build branching narrative story-based games, for example a choose your own adventure game where players have multiple choices on how to navigate the game. Within Twine there are several formats. The chosen format for this study is the Chapbook format, which is a new format in Twine that encourages simple ways to code, create, and learn. This study looks at whether Chapbook’s simple coding tools and easy-to-read commands help people better understand coding and feel more positive about learning to code.

Your participation will require one session lasting 45-60 minutes. This time will include a pre-survey, game play and a follow-up post survey. The pre-survey will take place via a Qualtrics survey, the game play will consist of playing a game, if in person on a provided computer, if on Zoom via an HTML download to your computer, and the final part of the study will be a post-survey via Qualtrics. You may participate either via Zoom from any location with internet access, or on campus. The researcher will be available throughout the session to answer questions.

First, you will take a brief survey (5-10 minutes) about your coding experience and your current thoughts about coding. Next, you will play Shopping Mall 1999 (20-30 minutes), an interactive story game that teaches basic coding concepts through exploring a 1990s mall with a guide cat named Oliver. After playing, you will complete a post-game survey (10-15 minutes) with the same questions from the first survey, plus three short-written questions about your experience and some basic demographic information.

Your participation in this study is voluntary. You are free to withdraw your consent and discontinue participation in this study at any time without prejudice or penalty. Your decision to participate or not participate in this study will in no way affect your relationship with UCF, including continued enrollment, grades, employment or your relationship with the individuals who may have an interest in this study.

Your information or samples that are collected as part of this research will not be used or distributed for future research studies, even if all of your identifiers are removed.
